Indie With Harmonies

The New Pornographers made you remember why group vocals matter

Broken Social Scene
"If you like The New Pornographers, try Broken Social Scene because they're basically the Canadian cousins with even more people on stage and somehow that chaos becomes beautiful orchestration."
Belle and Sebastian
"If you like The New Pornographers' sweetness, try Belle and Sebastian because Stuart Murdoch writes the kind of songs that make you want to wear cardigans and believe in love again."
The Shins
"If you like melodic indie pop, try The Shins because James Mercer crafts hooks that burrow into your brain and stay there for approximately seven years."
Of Montreal
"If you like experimental pop arrangements, try Of Montreal because Kevin Barnes creates the kind of art-rock that makes you question reality while dancing involuntarily."

Singer-Songwriter Folk

Iron & Wine proved you still have a heart under all that cynicism

Fleet Foxes
"If you like Iron & Wine's pastoral beauty, try Fleet Foxes because Robin Pecknold makes harmonies that sound like they were recorded in a cathedral made of trees."
Bon Iver
"If you like whispered folk confessions, try Bon Iver because Justin Vernon turned heartbreak into falsetto and somehow made isolation sound comforting."
Phoebe Bridgers
"If you like gentle devastation, try Phoebe Bridgers because she writes the kind of songs that make you want to lie on the floor and think about every mistake you've ever made, but in a good way."
Father John Misty
"If you like literary folk with bite, try Father John Misty because Josh Tillman combines Sam Beam's tenderness with the existential dread of someone who reads too much Cormac McCarthy."

Jam Band

You're going to multiple Phish and Dave shows, you beautiful deadhead

Widespread Panic
"If you like Dave Matthews Band's groove, try Widespread Panic because they're the southern rock answer to your jam band prayers, plus JB's guitar work will melt your face clean off."
Umphrey's McGee
"If you like Phish's technical prowess, try Umphrey's McGee because they took prog-rock ambition and gave it a jam band heart, which is like watching math become dancing."
The String Cheese Incident
"If you like eclectic jams, try The String Cheese Incident because they throw bluegrass into a blender with electronica and somehow it doesn't sound like a mistake."
Trey Anastasio
"If you like Phish but want smaller crowds, try Trey Anastasio solo because it's basically Phish with 80% less wookies and 100% more intimate venues."

British Alt

The Temper Trap reminded you that Australia counts as honorary Britain for indie purposes

Foals
"If you like The Temper Trap's anthemic builds, try Foals because they make math-rock accessible without dumbing it down, like if Radiohead learned to dance."
Two Door Cinema Club
"If you like upbeat indie rock, try Two Door Cinema Club because they took post-punk revival and made it actually fun instead of just fashionably miserable."
Franz Ferdinand
"If you like angular guitars, try Franz Ferdinand because they proved that art-rock could make people move their hips instead of just their heads."
Vampire Weekend
"If you like clever indie pop, try Vampire Weekend because Ezra Koenig writes songs that sound like they're about nothing and everything at the same time, which is basically the indie rock sweet spot."

Quiet Intensity

Band of Horses showed you that volume isn't everything

My Morning Jacket
"If you like Band of Horses' epic builds, try My Morning Jacket because Jim James has a voice that can whisper you to sleep or wake the dead, sometimes in the same song."
The War on Drugs
"If you like atmospheric rock, try The War on Drugs because Adam Granduciel makes heartland rock sound like it's being transmitted from space, in the best possible way."
Local Natives
"If you like harmony-driven indie rock, try Local Natives because they're what happens when five guys actually listen to each other instead of just waiting for their turn to solo."
Grizzly Bear
"If you like complex arrangements that sneak up on you, try Grizzly Bear because they make chamber pop that sounds like it was arranged by someone who actually understands how feelings work."
